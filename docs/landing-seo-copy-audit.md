# Landing Page SEO Copy Audit

## Primary search intent

The landing page should clearly answer searches such as:

- anonymous online communities
- online group chats
- interest-based communities
- anonymous community chat
- group chats for meeting like-minded people
- online communities for honest conversation

## High-impact wording changes implemented

| Area | Previous wording | Improved wording | Why |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Page title | `raW` | `Anonymous Online Communities & Group Chats \| raW` | Explains the product immediately and targets the main search intent. |
| Meta description | `Answer anonymously. Join the right 24/7 communities. Build your avatar identity.` | `Join anonymous online communities and 24/7 group chats built around honest conversation, shared interests, live polls, and real connection.` | Adds searchable product language and a clearer value proposition. |
| Hero description | `Your new 24/7 living and ever-growing world` | `Anonymous online communities for honest conversation and real connection` | Replaces vague branding language with a clear product explanation. |
| Hero support line | `Username And Password Only` | `Join interest-based group chats with only a username and password` | Preserves the privacy promise while explaining what visitors can join. |
| Problem heading | `The Problems raW Solves` | `Find online communities where you can be honest.` | Connects a user need with a searchable solution. |
| Communities heading | `24/7 communities for real talk.` | `Anonymous online communities and 24/7 group chats.` | Uses the strongest target phrase naturally. |
| Communities description | `Start with 3 founding categories...` | `Find interest-based community chats where you can speak honestly...` | Explains the experience and includes relevant search language. |
| How it works introduction | Product-development wording about a “shell” | Clear explanation of anonymous identity, polls, matching, communities, and group chats | Removes internal/product jargon that does not help visitors or search engines. |
| FAQ questions | Product-only questions | Questions about anonymous communities, finding group chats, and joining privately | Targets long-tail searches and explains the product to first-time visitors. |

## Recommended next content pages

The strongest next SEO improvement is to create focused, useful pages rather than repeating keywords on the homepage:

1. `/anonymous-online-communities` — explain how anonymous communities work, safety, privacy, and who they help.
2. `/online-group-chats` — explain active group chats, categories, moderation, and how to join.
3. `/interest-based-communities` — explain matching people through interests and poll answers.
4. Individual public community pages, such as `/communities/late-night-talks`, with unique descriptions and visible example topics.

Each page should answer real questions, show what makes the community useful, and link visitors to relevant community chats.

- Confirm `https://theartofraw.me/` is the intended canonical production domain.
- Submit `/sitemap.xml` in Google Search Console and Bing Webmaster Tools.
- Ensure important landing content is available without requiring visitors to complete the initial poll overlay.
- Add unique titles and meta descriptions for `/communities-explained` and `/polls-explained`.
- Measure impressions and queries in Search Console before expanding keyword targets.

## Supporting-page improvements implemented

- Added unique titles, descriptions, Open Graph text, Twitter text, and canonical URLs for the homepage, communities explainer, polls explainer, FAQ, security, privacy, and terms pages.
- Marked dashboard, pitch, test, assistant, unknown, and other non-public routes as `noindex`.
- Rewrote the communities explainer around clear terms such as “anonymous online communities,” “online group chat,” and “interest-based group chats.”
- Rewrote the polls explainer introduction to clearly connect anonymous polls with community discovery.
- Kept the full landing-page copy mounted behind the initial poll experience so crawlers can discover the actual page content without completing an interaction.
